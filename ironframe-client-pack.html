The Process

HOW IT WORKS.

Entirely remote. Minimal time from you after the onboarding call. Here's the process from first conversation to delivered documentation.

01
Discovery Call — 30 Minutes
A structured 30-minute call to understand your MSP's environment, your current documentation state, and your priorities. Audio only — no travel, no video required. We work from a structured intake questionnaire that gets everything we need in one session.
02
Intake Form Completion
After the call, you complete a detailed intake form covering your client environments, tool stack, processes, and team structure. Typically takes 45–60 minutes and can be done across multiple sittings. This is the foundation everything is built from.
03
Documentation Build — 5 Working Days
We build your complete documentation suite from your intake data. Every document is written specifically for your environment — not adapted from a template. Technical accuracy is reviewed against our infrastructure engineering background before delivery.
04
Review & Revision Round
You review the delivered documentation and flag any inaccuracies or additions. One full revision round is included. Most clients find 90%+ of the first delivery is accurate straight away — because the intake form does the heavy lifting.
05
Monthly Update Cycle — Ongoing
Submit changes via a simple shared form. New client onboarded? Infrastructure updated? New process introduced? Updates are delivered within 48 hours. Your documentation stays current without any effort from your team.

Market Context

WHY THIS MATTERS RIGHT NOW.

Documentation has always mattered. But three things happening in 2026 make it urgent in a way it hasn't been before.

CE
Cyber Essentials v3.3 — New Requirements Now Active
The 2026 CE update introduced mandatory MFA across all user accounts and a 14-day critical patching window. MSPs serving clients in regulated sectors are scrambling to produce evidence-ready documentation that proves compliance. An audit without documentation is a failed audit.
ICO
UK GDPR Enforcement Is Accelerating
ICO enforcement actions against SMBs and their service providers have increased sharply. A ransomware incident without a documented incident response procedure — and evidence it was followed — is now a serious regulatory exposure, not just an operational problem.
AI
Your Competitors Are Already Using AI — Are You Benefiting?
AI tools are changing what's possible in documentation speed and quality. MSPs that have clean, current documentation can leverage AI for faster troubleshooting, better client reporting, and faster onboarding. MSPs without it fall further behind. This gap is widening every quarter.

Common Questions

QUESTIONS ANSWERED.

How long does the first documentation build take? ›
First delivery is within 5 working days of receiving your completed intake form. For Full Suite engagements, we typically deliver in phases — core network docs and runbooks first, then onboarding handbook and incident response library within the first two weeks.
How much time does this require from my team? ›
After the initial 30-min discovery call and 45-60 min intake form (one-time), your ongoing commitment is minimal. Monthly updates: submit changes via a simple form — typically 15–20 minutes per month. Review of deliverables: 30–45 minutes per delivery. That's it. The heavy lifting is ours.
Is this just AI-generated content? ›
No. Every document is built from your actual environment data and reviewed by our infrastructure engineer before delivery. AI tools assist with drafting speed — the technical accuracy pass, the infrastructure-specific logic, the compliance-relevant content, and the quality review are all human. That's why we can guarantee it holds up under audit, and why our background in government-level infrastructure matters.
What format are documents delivered in? ›
Your choice — clean PDFs for each document, a structured Notion workspace for easy navigation and search, or both. Most clients take both: PDFs for formal records and audits, Notion for day-to-day technician access.
Can you document our client environments as well as our internal processes? ›
Yes. Full Suite and Operator tier cover both: your internal MSP operations (onboarding, incident response, runbooks) AND per-client network documentation for your managed clients. We scope the number of client environments during the discovery call — most MSPs in the 5–30 staff range have 10–40 managed clients.
What happens if our environment changes significantly? ›
That's exactly what the monthly update cycle is for. Major changes (e.g. migrating a client from on-prem to Azure, onboarding a new large client) may require a brief additional intake call — included at no extra cost. The retainer is designed so that documentation stays current regardless of how fast you grow.
We already have IT Glue / Hudu. Do we still need this? ›
IT Glue and Hudu are platforms — they store documentation. Ironframe IT creates the documentation that goes into them. Most of our clients use one of these platforms and we deliver content directly formatted for their chosen system. The tools don't write the runbooks. We do.
Can we cancel anytime? ›
After the initial 3-month minimum commitment, cancellation requires 30 days' written notice. All documentation produced during your engagement is yours to keep — no access restrictions, no platform fees, no conditions.
